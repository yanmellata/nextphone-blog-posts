## H2: Frequently Asked Questions (400-450 words)

### H3: Is Smith.ai really worth 3X the cost of AI receptionists?
For law firms handling complex personal injury or family law intake under 100 calls/month, yesthe human judgment and empathy can be worth the premium. For routine intake, scheduling, and high-volume scenarios, noAI delivers 90% of the value at 40% of the cost.

### H3: Can AI receptionists handle legal intake as well as Smith.ai?
For routine legal intake (contact info, case type, availability), AI performs identically. For complex emotional situations requiring empathy (trauma, grief, crisis), human receptionists have an edge. Most legal intake is routine enough for AI.

### H3: What happens when AI can't handle a call?
Modern AI receptionists include call transfer features. When AI encounters a situation beyond its capability, it seamlessly transfers to a human backup number you configure. This gives you the best of both worlds.

### H3: Does Smith.ai use AI at all?
Yes, Smith.ai is "hybrid"human receptionists use AI assistance tools for CRM lookup, script suggestions, and data entry. But humans are answering and making decisions, not AI.

### H3: How accurate is AI at understanding legal terminology?
When properly trained on your knowledge base, AI understands legal terms (plaintiff, defendant, statute of limitations, contingency fee) as well as humans. The challenge isn't vocabularyit's emotional intelligence and judgment in ambiguous situations.

### H3: Can I use both Smith.ai and AI together?
Absolutely. Many firms use AI (like NextPhone) for after-hours and overflow, then route complex calls to Smith.ai or in-house staff during business hours. This hybrid approach maximizes ROI.

### H3: Which integrates better with Clio?
Smith.ai has a native Clio integration that's plug-and-play. NextPhone can integrate with Clio via HTTP webhooks with slightly more setup. Both sync contact data and call notes effectively.

### H3: What if my call volume is unpredictable?
AI receptionists with unlimited plans (like NextPhone) are better for unpredictable volume. Smith.ai's per-call overage fees make busy months expensive and create budget anxiety.
