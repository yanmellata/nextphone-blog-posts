## SECTION 3: STIR/SHAKEN: Caller ID Verification Explained

**Purpose:** Explain STIR/SHAKEN technology in plain language
**Word Count Target:** 250-300 words

**H3 Subsections:**
1. What STIR/SHAKEN Does
2. How Caller ID Authentication Works
3. STIR/SHAKEN Limitations

**Key Points to Cover:**
- STIR/SHAKEN = digital certificate system for phone networks
- Verifies calls haven't been spoofed
- FCC mandated implementation by June 2021 for large carriers
- Authenticates the originating carrier (not the caller identity)
- Only works on IP networks (older systems need upgrades)
- Helps reduce spoofing but doesn't stop all spam
- Scammers from overseas not affected by US laws

**Data/Stats to Include:**
- **FCC mandate June 30, 2021** - Source: FCC call authentication page
- **"Authenticates originating carrier, not caller ID"** - Source: RoboKiller STIR/SHAKEN explainer

**Examples/Quotes:**

- Quote: "STIR/SHAKEN can't stop spam calls or punish scammers... Many spam calls originate from outside the United States"
