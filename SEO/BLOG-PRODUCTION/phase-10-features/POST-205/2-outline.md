## SECTION 7: COMPLIANCE AND CONSENT MANAGEMENT

**Purpose:** Address legal concerns in plain English
**Word Count Target:** 350-400 words

**H3 Subsections:**
1. Federal Law: One-Party Consent
2. State Laws: Two-Party Consent States
3. How to Stay Compliant (Simple Steps)
4. How NextPhone Handles Consent

**Key Points to Cover:**
- **Federal law:** One-party consent (you can record if you're part of the call) under 18 U.S. Code Section 2511
- **State laws:** 11 states require two-party/all-party consent (California, Delaware, Florida, Illinois, Maryland, Massachusetts, Montana, Nevada, New Hampshire, Pennsylvania, Washington)
- **FCC requirements:** Verbal notification at call start, written consent, or audible beep tone
- **Best practice:** Safest approach is to notify all parties ("This call may be recorded for quality assurance")
- **Interstate calls:** Follow the most restrictive law when callers are in different states
- **NextPhone approach:** Auto-plays consent message if enabled, compliant storage, easy to configure

**Data/Stats to Include:**
- **List:** Two-party consent states (11 states) - Source: Quo.com, Justia
- **Requirement:** FCC notification options - Source: Phonexa
