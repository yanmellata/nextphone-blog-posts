## How Call Transcription Works

Call transcription uses artificial intelligence to convert speech into text. Here's what happens behind the scenes.

### AI-Powered Speech Recognition

When a call comes in, the system captures the audio in real-time. AI analyzes the speech patterns using natural language processing (NLP)the same technology that powers voice assistants like Siri and Alexa.

The AI identifies individual words, understands context, and outputs a text version of the conversation with timestamps. The whole process takes seconds.

### Accuracy Rates (What to Expect)

[Modern AI transcription systems achieve 85-95% accuracy](https://aircall.io/call-center-software-features/call-transcription/) for routine business calls. That means most words are correct, with occasional errors on names, technical terms, or background noise.

What affects accuracy?

- **Call quality:** Clear audio = better transcription
- **Technical terminology:** "condenser coil" might become "condenser call" the first time
- **Speaker clarity:** Mumbling or heavy accents can reduce accuracy
- **Background noise:** Construction sites are harder than quiet offices

But here's the thing: 85% accuracy is more than enough to understand what the call was about. You're not using this in court transcripts. You're using it to know who called and why.

### Continuous Improvement Over Time

The [AI learns from each call](https://www.withallo.com/blog/how-to-turn-your-voicemails-to-text). If your business uses industry-specific terms"backflow preventer" for plumbers, "load-bearing wall" for contractorsthe AI gets better at recognizing these phrases over time.

This is why your transcripts get more accurate the longer you use the system.

## Compliance and Consent Management

Call recording is legal in most situations, but you need to follow the rules.

### Federal Law: One-Party Consent

Under [federal law (18 U.S. Code Section 2511)](https://www.justia.com/50-state-surveys/recording-phone-calls-and-conversations/), you can record a call if at least one party consents. Since you're part of the call (or your AI receptionist is acting on your behalf), you've got one-party consent covered.

This applies in 39 states.

### State Laws: Two-Party Consent States

Eleven states require all parties to consent before you can record:

- California
- Delaware
- Florida
- Illinois
- Maryland
- Massachusetts
- Montana
- Nevada
- New Hampshire
- Pennsylvania
- Washington

In these states, you must notify the caller that the call is being recorded. The [FCC requires](https://phonexa.com/blog/fcc-call-recordings-compliance/) one of the following:

- Prior verbal or written consent
- Verbal notification at the start of the call ("This call may be recorded...")
- An audible beep tone repeated during the call

### How to Stay Compliant (Simple Steps)

The safest approach: notify everyone. Add an auto-greeting that says:

"Thank you for calling [Your Business]. This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes."

Most people expect this. It's standard for businesses. And it covers you in all 50 states.

If your business operates in multiple states or takes calls from customers in two-party consent states, follow the most restrictive law. Better safe than sorry.
