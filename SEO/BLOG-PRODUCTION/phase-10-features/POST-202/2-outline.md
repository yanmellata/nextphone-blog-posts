## SECTION 4: The BANT Framework (and Its Limitations)

**H2 Title:** Traditional Lead Qualification: The BANT Framework

**Purpose:** Explain BANT basics and why customization is needed
**Word Count Target:** 250 words

**H3 Subsections:**
1. What is BANT?
2. BANT for Small Businesses
3. When BANT Isn't Enough

**Key Points to Cover:**
- BANT = Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline (developed by IBM in 1950s)
- Budget: Can they afford your service?
- Authority: Are they the decision-maker?
- Need: Do they have a problem you can solve?
- Timeline: When do they need it?
- BANT works for many businesses BUT needs customization
- One-size-fits-all BANT fails for specialized industries

**Data/Stats to Include:**
- **Stat:** "Average buying committees consist of 10-11 stakeholders, sales cycles stretched 20%" - Source: Monday.com
- Context: BANT's "Authority" question is more complex now
- **Quote:** "BANT should be customized based on your industry, buyer sophistication, and deal complexity" - Source: Rep.ai

**Examples/Quotes:**
- Example: Roofing contractor BANT customization
  - Budget: "What's your budget range for this project?" ’ "Is this an insurance claim or out-of-pocket?"
  - Authority: "Are you the homeowner?"
  - Need: "Repair or full replacement?"
  - Timeline: "Is this an emergency (active leak) or can it wait?"
