## ‚úçÔ∏è Writing Guidelines Summary

### Voice
- **Conversational consultant** - confident but not arrogant
- **Direct and helpful** - no fluff, no corporate jargon
- **Data-driven** - lead with proprietary stats
- **Honest** - address AI limitations, customer concerns

### Forbidden Phrases (AI Tell-Tales)
‚ùå "It is important to note that..."
‚ùå "In today's digital landscape..."
‚ùå "At the end of the day..."
‚ùå "Leveraging cutting-edge solutions..."
‚ùå "Revolutionize your business..."

### Structure
- Max 2-4 sentences per paragraph
- Active voice ("AI answers calls" not "calls are answered by AI")
- Short paragraphs alternating with medium
- Bullet lists for scanability
- Bold key phrases sparingly

### Data Citation
‚úÖ "In our analysis of 13,175 calls from 45 contractors..."
‚úÖ "We analyzed 7 months of call data and found that 74.1%..."
‚ùå "Studies show..." (too vague)
‚ùå "Many businesses lose..." (not specific)

## ‚ùì FAQ

**Q: Do I need to read all 1,000+ lines of Master Plan?**
A: No. Read BLOG_PRODUCTION_SYSTEM.md first (5 min), then skim Master Plan for context.

**Q: Can I skip research phase and go straight to writing?**
A: No. Research informs outline, outline guides writing. Skipping = poor quality + hours of rewriting.

**Q: How do I know if my writing sounds "AI-generated"?**
A: Read aloud. If it sounds robotic or uses forbidden phrases, rewrite. Check Phase 3 prompt for 15+ examples.

**Q: Can I batch multiple posts at once?**
A: Research phase can be batched (5 posts at once). Outline and writing should be one at a time for quality.

**Q: What if I find better data while writing?**
A: Add it! Phases are guidelines, not rigid rules. Better data = better post.
